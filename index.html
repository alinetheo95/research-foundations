Historical Lineage

Plants react to environmental factors and are all connected through a network in which they send signals to each other.

Mycelium is a hidden dimension of natural networks that exists within the roots of fungi and plants. Mycelium forms mycorrhizal networks among plants and fungi, facilitating water and nutrient exchange through electric signaling.

This network is referred to as the WWW - World Wide Web, often compared to the internet in terms of communication among networks.

Rhetorical Argument

Understanding mycorrhizal networks is crucial to addressing climate change. These networks risk reduction and worsened conditions as effects of climate change.

Using the rate of relationships from other networks, we can draw conclusions on mycorrhizal networks and apply them to other systems.

Plants “learn” just like AI systems do. If we can take what we know about training AI systems and apply it to plant intelligence, we have the opportunity to produce a great extent of new discovery.
