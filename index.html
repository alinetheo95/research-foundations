Historical Lineage

Plants react to environmental factors and are all connected through a network in which they send signals to each other.

Mycelium is a hidden dimension of natural networks that exists within the roots of fungi and plants. Mycelium forms mycorrhizal networks among plants and fungi, facilitating water and nutrient exchange through electronic signaling.

This network is referred to as the WWW - World Wide Web, often compared to the internet in terms of communication among networks.

Methodology

I plan to use plant sensors to collect data from plant signals and growth. This data can be analyzed through different types of visualization that can be projection mapped onto a physical space, beginning to blend the realms of the digital and physical environments.

Augmented reality programming is another tool that can also be utilized in intersecting digital and physical forms. This tool can be used to visualize “hidden” networks.

Computational Design Experiments

I’m interested in exploring networks, differential growth, and water properties in order to investigate visualizing the unseen communications in natural systems.

In other classes I have been focusing on flooding analysis due to climate change, as well as mapping different types of networks. Through computational techniques, I'd like to further explore the relationships and reactivities between growth, water conditions, and environments.

Rhetorical Argument

Understanding mycorrhizal networks is crucial to addressing climate change. These networks risk reduction and worsened conditions as effects of climate change.

Using the rate of relationships from other networks, we can draw conclusions on mycorrhizal networks and apply them to other systems.

Plants “learn” just like AI systems do. If we can take what we know about training AI systems and apply it to plant intelligence, we have the opportunity to produce a great extent of new discovery.

Capstone Project Foundations

For my capstone project, I’d like to expand upon the existing biotechnology research on the intersection of the natural environment and technology. I’ll be looking at examples of electronic and robotic plant experiments that are already underway and letting further research inform the direction of this project.

I’ll be making observations and collecting data to distinguish how these systems can be applied at larger scales, whether for climate change intervention, or other environmental benefits, depending on what further research shows.

In considering plant intelligence, I’ll be examining the way that natural systems react to different conditions and what we can learn from them.
